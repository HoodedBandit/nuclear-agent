# Dashboard Visual Concepts

## Concept A: Instrument Wall

- Strong vertical nav rail, segmented command shelves, and a right-side telemetry inspector.
- Dense framed modules with thin borders, minimal rounding, and emphasis on scan speed.
- Best for operational seriousness, but risked feeling too austere during longer sessions.

## Concept B: Tactical Console

- Layered bridge-like shell with a top command deck, angled sectional dividers, and a live event ribbon.
- Strongest visual identity and the least likely to resemble a generic AI dashboard.
- Risk was over-stylization if the surfaces became decorative rather than useful.

## Concept C: Systems Atlas

- Broader canvas with map-like panel zoning, large summary tables, and subsystem clustering.
- Excellent for dense inspection workflows and wide monitors.
- Risk was weaker focus for the primary task flow compared to a cockpit layout.

## Selected Direction

Use **Concept B: Tactical Console** as the base, borrowing the panel readability of Concept A and the zoning discipline of Concept C.

### Why it wins

- Most distinctive without relying on novelty gimmicks.
- Strong operator hierarchy at a glance.
- Supports dense data without collapsing into a card farm.
- Fits the product goal of a premium resident control room rather than a generic admin console.

## Visual Acceptance Checklist

- The shell reads as a custom cockpit within the first second.
- Primary status, chat, and system state remain legible at high information density.
- Surfaces use crisp framing and segmentation rather than soft rounded-card repetition.
- Accent colors stay functional: cyan for live/system state, amber for caution, reactor green for success, red for danger.
- Tablet layout preserves hierarchy without turning into stacked generic cards.
